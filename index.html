Weather Patterns Across Australia's Capital Cities and Their Potential Impact on Energy Consumption

Singapore, a compact island nation, is known for its consistent tropical climate, where temperatures hover around a steady 28°C year-round in all of its regions. You might think this uniformity is typical of all countries, where cities and regions share similar weather conditions, creating a cohesive experience for residents and visitors alike.

However, Australia offers a completely different story. Spanning an immense landmass with diverse terrains and climates, Australia’s cities and states can exhibit vastly different weather at the same time.
Imagine basking in the tropical heat of Darwin while just 500 kilometers away in Hobart, cold winds blow in from the Antarctic. This striking contrast highlights the unique nature of Australia's climate.

In this visualization, we’ll explore these fascinating weather patterns across Australia’s major cities, comparing different aspects of climate to uncover the diversity that makes this country truly remarkable.
